Sometimes a visual artifact helps us understand a problem. A mind map is a tool I like for getting stuff out of my head. How to mind map:

**1) Start with a central idea**. Maybe it’s a theme, a single word, a problem, an image, an experience, a feeling.

<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter">![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa6d4c990-48c8-4fac-8f34-a6c6b536ff8e_325x267.png)</figure></div>[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa6d4c990-48c8-4fac-8f34-a6c6b536ff8e_325x267.png)**2) Draw related ideas**. Use Why? What If? How? as a starting point.

<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter">![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb331a47-9664-4974-8499-2e0cd73ec9f0_601x487.png)</figure></div>[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb331a47-9664-4974-8499-2e0cd73ec9f0_601x487.png)**3) Draw secondary and tertiary ideas**. Think of each sub-idea as the new center.

<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter">![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1d42c3e5-6967-4f39-a910-d6df1b5f0ee8_774x489.png)</figure></div>[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1d42c3e5-6967-4f39-a910-d6df1b5f0ee8_774x489.png)**4) Connect ideas with lines**. Do two unrelated things inspire a new concept to join them?

<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter">![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff92b8782-d800-47b9-a0b5-3936c0fb2ea7_750x341.png)</figure></div>[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff92b8782-d800-47b9-a0b5-3936c0fb2ea7_750x341.png)**5) Embrace curves and color**. Abandoning straight lines can relax your mind. Don’t be afraid to change an idea’s color or size.

<div class="wp-block-image"><figure class="aligncenter">![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F11ab545b-e13e-4eaa-b8ef-aaf17523e80a_666x370.png)</figure></div>[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F11ab545b-e13e-4eaa-b8ef-aaf17523e80a_666x370.png)**6) Step back**. You might have started with “faster horses,” but your exploration might have surfaced a new center—a more alluring idea.
